Before You Begin
There's something I need
you to know first.

This wasn't written in one sitting. This wasn't easy. And this definitely wasn't something I planned to ever send you. Because I know doing so will lead us to somewhere we don't want to be.

And I've started writing this so many times… and every single time, I stopped halfway. Not because I didn't have anything to say, but because I had too much to say, and no courage to actually let you read it. Because once you read this… things might change between us.

Because what we have right now, even if it's undefined for me, even if it's confusing for me sometimes, it still means something to me. And the thought of losing that just because I couldn't keep my feelings to myself… it kept me quiet for a long time... Yup for a really long time.

"Some feelings don't go away just because you ignore them. They stay. They grow. And at some point… they stop asking for permission and start demanding to be heard."

So this… this is me finally listening to them. I don't expect anything from you after this. I'm writing this because if I don't say it now, I might never say it at all and I might really regret not saying this. And I know you don't feel the same for the way I feel for you and I don't hate you for that because it's me who's fallen for you you don't have to feel guilty or blame yourself for this. It was all me.

And maybe this letter will mean nothing to you.

Or maybe it will mean something.

Or maybe it will just be a memory we never talk about again.

But whatever happens after this…

just know that every word you're about to read is real and written with care.

Unnoticeable · Chapter Two
I don't know
when it started.

That's the most honest answer I can give you. Because there wasn't a moment where everything suddenly changed. No exact day. No specific conversation. It was quieter. It happened in pieces… in moments so small that I didn't even notice them while they were happening.

At first, it was just curiosity. You were someone I didn't fully understand, and for some reason, that made me want to understand you more. Not in a surface-level way… but deeply. I wanted to know the versions of you that people don't usually see.

"You started becoming a part of my thoughts in ways that didn't make sense. I'd be doing something completely normal… and somehow, you'd still find your way into it. Not loudly. Not forcefully. Just… there."

I tried to ignore it. I really did. But no matter how much I tried to push it away… it didn't leave. It stayed. And slowly… it started growing into something I couldn't deny anymore.

The truth is… I was lying to myself. Because no matter how much I denied it, my actions kept proving otherwise. The way I noticed the smallest things about you. The way I remembered things you said without even trying.

"This isn't something I can control. This isn't something I chose. I didn't plan this. I didn't expect this. And I definitely didn't want to fall for you this way. But somehow… I still did."

Enough For Me · Chapter Four
I just want to
see you happy.

And I know… that sounds like something people say all the time. But I don't mean it in a simple way. I mean it in the kind of way where your happiness doesn't have to include me… and it would still matter to me just as much.

I remember that moment so clearly. When Hamza gave those Kashmiri bangles to Taiba… and then you borrowed one just to wear it. It was such a small thing. So normal. But I couldn't forget it.

It wasn't anger. It wasn't exactly sadness either. It was just… this sudden tightness in my chest. Like something inside me shifted — quietly, but deeply — in a way I wasn't prepared for.

"It broke something in me. Just a little. Not because they were there for you — you deserve people like that. But because I realized… I wasn't. Not in the way I wanted to be."

Still… I don't hate that feeling. Because at the end of it — you're smiling. And somehow… that still reaches me. I don't want to be someone who complicates your life. I want to make things lighter.

"Being something in your life — no matter how small — still means more to me than being everything in someone else's."

No Regrets · Chapter Five
And I don't
regret it.

Not the confusion. Not the overthinking. Not even the parts that hurt more than I expected them to. Because feeling all of this for you — even the complicated parts — is still something I wouldn't trade for anything simpler.

I'm not ashamed of caring about you this much. I'm not ashamed of thinking about you more than I probably should. If anything… I'm grateful for it. Because in a way — you made me feel something genuine. Something unfiltered. Something I didn't have to fake or force or question every second.

"If I had the choice to go back… to feel nothing… to never notice you the way I did… I wouldn't take it. Because even if this stays one-sided… it was still real to me."

Not the late-night thoughts.

Not the moments where I caught myself smiling for no reason.

Not even the times where it hurt more than it should've.

Because every part of it… led me here.

And that's enough for me to never regret it.

Final Chapter · Always
I love you.

And I guess this is the part I kept avoiding the most. Not because I didn't know what to say… but because I knew exactly what it meant once I said it. Because some words… once they're out — they don't go back to being just thoughts. They become real.

"I love you. And I don't mean that in a rushed way. Or in a momentary way. Or in a way that expects something back. I mean it in the quiet way it grew inside me."

In the way I started noticing the smallest things about you without even realizing when it began. In the way your mood affects mine even when I try not to let it. In the way I find myself wanting things to be easier for you. It's not loud. It's not dramatic. It's just… there. Steady. Constant. Real.

And I know — this might not be what you expected. And that's okay. I'm not saying this to change anything between us. I'm saying it because it's the truth. And you deserve honesty. Nothing more than that.

"What you feel — what you choose — what this means to you — that's completely yours. I'm not here to take that away from you."

Because at least now — you know.

And maybe that's all I needed in the end.

Not an answer. Not a promise.

Just the chance to finally say it…

without holding anything back.

Epilogue · Our Memories
The moments I keep
coming back to.

I remember countless weddings we met at, how we'd both steal glances at each other, pretending it was casual, pretending it didn't matter — when secretly I was obsessed with the way you looked. Those moments — our eyes meeting across a crowded room, the tiny, unspoken smiles — felt like sparks in my chest.

And then came the last wedding. When you finally said you would come, I was genuinely amazed. That small miracle of your presence made my heart swell. Then came the custard — made without a second thought because you asked. Watching you eat it, seeing your face light up, filled me with a warmth that was almost unbearable.

Even the smallest moments — your eyes crinkling in laughter, your soft sighs, the casual brush of your hand — felt sacred. The world itself seemed smaller, quieter, safer, and yet fuller whenever you were near.
